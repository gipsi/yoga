A Short Yoga Vocabulary

Glossary of yoga terms useful to learn whilst practising Yoga with Adriene

Adho Mukha Svanasana

Downward Facing Dog is an inverted v posture with both palms and feet on the ground. Builds strength in the upper body and increases flexibility.

Ananda Balasana

Happy baby pose.

Asana

The word used for a yoga pose or posture.

Bandha

Body lock used to control the flow of energy through the body.

Chakra

One of seven spinning energy points aligned along the spine.

Chaturanga

Slowly lowering to the ground from plank position.

Danda

A straight rod, stick or staff of energy from crown to tail bone.

Drishti

Focused gaze on a particular spot, meant to draw awareness, concentration, and intent to yoga practice.

Kumbhaka

Retention of breath means pot as in vessel filled with air. Wikipedia

Mantra

Words or phrases that are repeated.

Mudra

Bring thumb and forefinger together while keeping the other three fingers out straight. Jnana mudra hand gesture is used in meditation because it’s known as the gesture of knowledge.

Namaste

'The divine in me acknowledges the divine in you.'

Om

Is made up of three separate sounds, aah, ooo and mm.

Pranayama

One of the eight limbs of yoga intended to expand prana, the vital air that permeates everything.

Savasana

Corpse Pose is the blissful state of relaxation or meditation, usually at the end of yoga session.

Sukhasana

The pose of ease is the basic sitting cross legged yoga posture.

Vinyasa

Yoga poses that flow seamlessly from one to another synchronised with the breath.

Surya Namaskara

Sun salutation is a series of movements and yoga flows incorporating every organ and system in the body. .

Tadasana

Mountain pose, stand tall like a mountain!

Ujjayi

Ocean breath or warrior’s breath involves breathing in and out of the nose with deep inhales and exhales.

Utkata Konasana

Is the Fierce angled or Goddess pose balancing strength and power of feminine energy with the inner wisdom of breath.

Utkatasana

IsChair pose but it could also be Throne as the translation can mean fierce, proud, high, haughty, superior, immense, large or difficult".

Uttanasana

Forward fold,standing forward bend orIntense stretch pose. From Tadasana, Mountain pose, bend softly forward at the hips, gently wiggling the fingers until the palms can be placed on the floor, eventually behind the heels.

    variations include:
  • Halfway lift or Ardha Uttanasana has the torso horizontal and the palms resting on the calves or Aeroplane arms stretched horizontally behind.
  • Niralamba Uttanasana has the hands touching the waistband rather than reaching down.
  • Padahastasana has the hands under the toes and feet, palms up.
